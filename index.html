Born-Haber Cycle Interactive Activity

The Born-Haber cycle is a thermodynamic approach used to calculate lattice energy by breaking down the formation of an ionic solid into a series of individual steps. Each step represents a specific energy change that can be measured or calculated, allowing us to apply Hess's Law to determine the overall lattice energy.

Born-Haber Cycle Step Order:

  1. Enthalpy of Formation: The overall energy change when the ionic compound forms from its elements.
  2. Enthalpy of Vaporization: Energy required to convert the metal from solid to gas phase.
  3. Ionization Energy: Energy required to remove an electron from the gaseous metal atom.
  4. Bond Dissociation Energy: Energy required to break the diatomic non-metal molecule into individual atoms.
  5. Electron Affinity: Energy released when the gaseous non-metal atom gains an electron.
  6. Lattice Enthalpy: Energy released when gaseous ions combine to form the ionic solid.

Remember: According to Hess's Law, the sum of all individual steps equals the enthalpy of formation!
